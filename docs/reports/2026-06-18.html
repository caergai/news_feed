The award is described as one of the world's most prestigious honors, emphasizing the global importance of the Muon g-2 results.

Nine projects selected to ignite Discovery supercomputer's day-one science mission.

On June 17, 2026, researchers at the Department of Energy's Oak Ridge National Laboratory selected the first nine research projects to execute on the Discovery supercomputer. These applications are designated for "day-one science," representing the inaugural set of computational workloads that will utilize the system immediately upon availability. This selection marks a critical operational milestone, shifting focus from hardware readiness to the immediate advancement of scientific discovery across nine distinct domains.

The selection of day-one science projects accelerates the transition of next-generation supercomputing infrastructure into active research, enabling immediate high-impact discoveries and reinforcing the strategic importance of high-performance computing in addressing complex global challenges.
